Kerry L. Shaw - Professor  - Contact: kls4<at>cornell.edu

My lab studies speciation in a radiation of Hawaiian swordtail crickets (genus Laupala) that shows one of the highest speciation rates in the animal world. Our research stretches from behavioral and quantitative genetics, to examining the evolution of pre and postcopulatory isolation, to inferring the phylogeographic history of closely related but sexually divergent cricket species.

Mingzi Xu - Postdoc- Contact: mx52<at>cornell.edu

I am interested in the mechanisms driving co-evolution of sexual traits and preferences. I use high resolution quantitative trait locus mapping to dissect the genomic location and causal genes underlying song and song preference variation in Laupala kohalensis and L. paranigra.

Thomas Blankers - Postdoc- Contact: tb492<at>cornell.edu. thomasblankers.com

I am interested in the evolutionary genetics of speciation. I use comparative quantitative genetics and population genetics approaches to understand the relationship between genetic architecture of speciation phenotypes, the geographic and demographic context of genetic evolution, and the effects of (sexual) selection.

Glenn Stamps - Grad student- Contact: gfs52<at>cornell.edu.

I am interested in the role of chemical communication in mate choice and speciation. I examine the variation in cuticular hydrocarbon (CHCs) and their contribution to premating isolation in Laupala.

I am interested in behavioral isolation, especially at the mechanistic level of analysis.
